Exchanges are the biggest HODLers
Using our new endpoints, we visualize the relationship between exchange balances and BTC price and discovered interesting trends. 
Key insights:
  • 6.7% (~$9.8B) of all BTC in circulation is currently held on exchange wallets.
  • Through price spikes and crashes, the stockpile of BTC on exchange wallets has been has been increasing consistently.
Zooming over the past year, we see more interesting trends.
Key insights:
  • BTC held on exchanges increases more distinctly when customers sell their cryptoassets during price drops.
  • Similarly decreasing amounts of BTC held on exchanges signal increased buying by customers (seen during price rallies).
  • We can see this behaviour between Mar 2019 and May 2019, as balance on exchange wallets decreases, prices sharply rise. Aka more Bitcoin is bought on exchanges and taken out during the price rally.
  • Ultimately these minor course corrections don't impact the larger trend of BTC held in exchange wallets growing over time.

Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.

Bitcoin
  • On 10/07 the BTC price rebounds by ~5% along with a net outflow of ~25M$.
  • On 10/07 and 10/08 we had a total of 50M$ net outflow, indicating traders buying BTC on exchanges and taking it out.

Ethereum
  • The ETH prices are strongly correlated to BTC prices, following the BTC price rebound on the 10/07.
  • On 10/07 and 10/08 we had a total of $20M net inflow, which still didn't make a dent in the BTC-ETH price correlation.

Stablecoins
  • Unusually large outflow on 10/06 sets an increasing trend in daily flows prior to the large spike in BTC & ETH prices on 10/07
  • Investors have withdrawn $100M worth of stablecoins from exchanges indicating that they are not using stablecoins to buy other cryptoassets.

Overview
  • We observe an overall decrease in inflow for native tokens with BTC down -22% and ETH down -10%.
  • Stablecoins show a similar trend to native tokens with USDT Omni down -54% and USDT ERC20 down -38%.
