TokenAnalyst Engineering: A look at what we've been working on

 

@madewithtea writes about blockchain-rpc - our open-source RPC client that we wrote in-house for efficiently extracting and parsing on-chain data. Read about it here and give it a star on github.

Highlights
  • blockchain-rpc is a JSON RPC client that currently supports Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Omni and is written for Scala 2.12 or 2.13.
  • Instead of relying on RPC libraries for the different chains (mostly in untyped Javascript), we decided to build a thin layer library called blockchain-rpc to only connect, download and parse raw data into abstract data types (without interpretation) in a typesafe Scala way.
  • The library is very generic since we're using blockchain-dependent type class instances, so in theory, one can snap any type of block-transaction based blockchain into this syntax.

USDT and USDC held on exchanges crossed $1B this week

 

@ankitchiplunkar visualizes the balance of USDT and USDC held on exchange wallets and shows how it crossed $1B this week.

Key Insights
  • With turmoil in the global markets, there has been a mass exodus from volatile crypto-assets to stablecoins, with USDT and USDC being the most popular stablecoins.
  • Instead of withdrawing these stablecoins from exchanges, traders and investors are holding them on exchanges, waiting for some form of sign (positive or negative) to switch back into BTC or other volatile cryptoassets.
  • This indicates the potential for abrupt and intense volatility once this stablecoin capital is traded for BTC, ETH or other volatile assets.

Community Insights

 

@ceterispar1bus outlines how Bitcoin's rolling 7-day hashrate had the largest two week drop in history over these past 2 weeks.

Key Insights
  • As the price of BTC dropped, we at TokenAnalyst started seeing much slower block times as miners started pulling the plug on hardware as breakeven levels didn't look likely in the near-term future. 
  • Concurrently, we saw Bitcoin's mining difficulty decreased by ~15% this past week.
  • This is a natural adjustment of the Bitcoin protocol to ensure 10 minute block times as the network hashrate changes.
  • Miner 'capitulation' and the pricing in of the upcoming halving in 44 days are nuanced topics that don't have a one-size-fits-all answer. To draw your own conclusions, feel free to refer to our miner stats dashboard.

Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.

Overview
  • Both inflows and outflows into exchanges are down this week compared to last week across the board. Following on from last week, and as shown in the exchange balance charts above, this points to a large portion of trading capital currently sitting on exchanges.
  • One data point that sticks out here is the increase in average exchange outflow amounts for non-USDT stablecoins. This suggests larger players withdrawing funds for use cases outside of trading/speculation or simply to hold and weather the storm as the USD remains a safe-haven asset to hold right now outside of the crypto ecosystem (despite the money printer going brrr).
