Miner revenue in USD reduced by 60%

Highlights

  • Exchange SOPR: Exchange users sold BTC at a loss last week
  • Miner balances: Bitcoin miners are not capitulating
  • On-chain network congestion: 60% drop in BTC block times due to the price drop
  • Exchange stats: Supply and demand for crypto assets
  • Network stats: Usage metrics of native tokens, Stablecoins, and ERC20s

Exchange users sold Bitcoins at a loss last week

 

@simonohanlon101 investigates Spent Outputs Profit Ratio (SOPR) specific to exchanges during the price crash last week.

Follow the twitter thread for the full story.

Key Insights
  • During the period March 8th to March 14th the bitcoin flowing into each exchange from consumer wallets was worth an average of 1.5 - 7.7% less than the price paid for it.
  • Overall bitcoin sent to Deribit had the highest losses with SOPR of 0.93.
  • In particular, there were 11 deposits into OKEx controlled wallets totaling around 23k of BTC which had a mean created price of $12,815 and a mean deposited price of $6,459.

Bitcoin Miners are not capitulating!

 

@jai_prasad17 visualizes miner balances and implies that miners are not capitulating.

Follow the twitter thread for the full story.

Key Insights
  • Miner balances are not decreasing despite the sudden drop in BTC prices due to COVID-19
  • Miner BTC inventory is in the 10's of thousand BTC. 2 orders of magnitude lower than $BTC held on exchanges!!

60% drop in BTC block times due to the price drop

 

We visualize the slowdown in BTC block times in the last 2 weeks due to the price drop.

Follow the twitter thread for the full story.

Key Insights
  • USD denominated block reward dropped by 60% in the last 2 weeks. The average daily block time has increased by 60%
  • To cope with reduced revenue, miners switch off equipment to reduce costs, resulting in increased block times.

Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.

Overview
  • This week has reduced volatility when compared to last week. Decreased volatility led to reduced exchange flows.
  • Average daily inflows are down by 45% for BTC, USDT and ETH while average daily outflows are down by 30% for BTC, USDT and ETH.
  • Traders have bought and withdrawn $340M worth of BTC from exchanges last week.
