Spike in BTC addresses holding more than 1 BTC

 

@jai_prasad17 investigates the relation between BTC price and addresses holding more than 1 BTC.
Highlights
  • BTC price and addresses containing more than 1 BTC are positively correlated
  • Selling of BTC by retail causes a reduction in number of individual addresses and also a decrease in the price.

Community Insights

 

@eliasimos uses TokenAnalyst PRO to show how stablecoins are being issued and used on-chain. 

Key Insights
  • $2.2B worth of stablecoins have been printed between 1st March and 2nd April 2020.
  • A total of $350M worth of stablecoins went into exchanges between 15th March and 2nd April 2020.
  • 95% of those flows are due to USDT ERC20.

 

@ceterispar1bus uses TokenAnalyst PRO to show how the amount of BTC going out of BitMEX since 13th of March.
 
Key Insights
  • BitMEX is one of the exchanges that has seen it's BTC balance drop significantly after the debacle on March 12th - with almost 3x as withdrawals as other exchanges.
  • Since its high of 315k BTC the BitMEX wallet balance has reduced by 25% in a span of 3 weeks.

Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.

Overview
  • A total of $100M worth of BTC went out of exchanges whereas a total of $120M worth of stablecoins went into exchanges. Suggesting that traders are buying BTC for stablecoins.
  • Average daily inflow into exchanges grew by 56% for ETH while average daily outflow grew by 36%.
