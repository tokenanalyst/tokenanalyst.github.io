USDT on Ethereum

We've been hearing a lot of chatter about the impact of the USDT ERC20 token on the Ethereum blockchain, so we decided to take a quick look

Key insights:

  • USDT_ERC20 has overtaken ETH, the native token of the Ethereum blockchain, in on-chain volume (USD). In-fact, USDT_ERC20 had a $1.6B on-chain volume day this week.
  • Note: ETH volume is sum of external and internal ETH transactions. More info about this distinction in our docs.
  • USDT_ERC20 volume is orders of magnitude greater than the volume of other stablecoins on Ethereum,
  • It's also on average 50% larger than USDT_OMNI volume on the OMNI layer. What is interesting though is that on-chain volume of the USDT_ERC20 and USDT_Omni follow a very similar pattern.
  • What is generating all this volume you might ask (it rhymes with Finance)? Go see for yourself in our ERC20 exchange flows API
Check out ERC20 Exchange flows
  • Given the high volume, you'd expect USDT_ERC20 transaction count to surpass that of ETH (we only measure transactions where the amount of ETH transferred is > 0), but that's not the case. ETH is still the most transacted asset on the Ethereum blockchain.
  • This is explained because USDT_ERC20 is primarily being used today for trading and transferring funds among exchanges, and the typical user is most likely a large fund or trader, thus the average value per USDT transaction is still relatively high.

Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.

Bitcoin
  • As in previous weeks, we can see that in the days directly following negative BTC netflows (more BTC taken out of exchanges than deposited), prices see a positive surge.

Ethereum
  • As ETH price surged this week, overall flow activity rose as well.
  • We see inflows now rising as well, which hints at some potential selling in the following days as investors realize some of their gains.

Stablecoins

Overview
  • BTC has dipped this week across the board - from price to inflows to outflows. The average transaction value has remained quite stable throughout the past 2 weeks however.
  • ETH has a seen a rise in outflows as well as average transaction values as prices surged this week
  • USDT ERC20 and Dai are the most interesting stablecoins this week as significantly increased inflow was seen into exchanges this week compared to last week.
