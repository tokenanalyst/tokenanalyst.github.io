Two new API endpoints, Data Catalogue added to API and Visualizing Bitcoin Price and Deribit Exchange Flows

Highlights

  • TokenAnalyst Updates: Two new API endpoints added, improvements to Bitcoin miner wallet identification and Data Catalogue added to our API
  • Visualizing Bitcoin Price and Deribit Exchange Flows: Visualizing the Bitcoin price crash & Deribit Exchange Flows on Jan 18, 2020.
  • Exchange stats: Supply and demand for crypto assets
  • Network stats: Usage metrics of native tokens, Stablecoins, and ERC20s

TokenAnalyst Updates

Bitcoin Total Hashrate & Total Rewards endpoints added

  • We've added Bitcoin Total Hashrate and Total Rewards endpoints to our API for you to monitor the daily hashrate of miners and the daily rewards paid out. Full documentation access is available here.

Improved Bitcoin miner wallet identification

  • In preparation of the halvening, we have significantly improved our BTC miner wallet identification algorithms, adding 145K more identified miner addresses and 90 more unique miners/pools to our coverage.
  • This will impact historical data for our miner endpoints (hashrate, rewards, and miner flows).
  • Below is a chart visualizing our coverage of the total hashrate of known Bitcoin miners.

Data Catalogue added to our API

  • We have also added a Data Catalog functionality to our API that allows you to programmatically see all the available endpoints that you can query (the various combinations of tokens, metrics, and entities).
  • This makes looping through all the tokens/exchanges/miners for any given endpoint, in order to derive an aggregate metric a lot easier.

  Visualizing Bitcoin Price and Deribit Exchange Flows

 

Using TokenAnalyst Insights we had a look at the BTC price crash on January 18th, 2020 and found some interesting trends.

Check out our Twitter thread for more insights.

Key Facts
  • The only significant transaction that preceded this move was a 400 BTC outflow from Deribit at 9am UTC.
  • All other exchanges have peaks in outflow at ~11am UTC.

Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.

Overview
  • We observe a significant increase in the outflow amount of BTC by 19%. Interestingly, ~$119M worth of BTC went out of exchanges on Jan 18.
  • $53M worth of USDT ERC20 went into exchanges this week, indicating traders are buying crypto-assets during a volatile atmosphere.
  • The inflow and outflow amounts of PAX and TUSD are down significantly, indicating reduced usage for these tokens.
