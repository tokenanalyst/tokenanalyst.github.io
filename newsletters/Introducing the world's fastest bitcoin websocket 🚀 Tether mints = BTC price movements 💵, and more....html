Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.

Bitcoin
  • There was a tremendous spike in outflow on 09/27, followed by an increase in BTC price.
  • A large BTC price spike occured on 09/30 due to Tether minting an additional 20M USDT on the same day.

Ethereum
  • Large outflow on 09/26 just as the price began to dip sharply as traders purchased ETH from exchanges.
  • Similar to BTC, towards the start of the week a large rebound in ETH price as well as increases in daily flow due to Tether minting.
  • Although a huge outflow occurred on 09/26, ETH prices dipped due to a strong correlation with BTC price. 

Stablecoins
  • As expected, stablecoin outflow increased on 09/25 during a BTC price decline as traders sold BTC for stablecoins.
  • At the start of the week, larger daily flows are aligned to the increase in BTC and ETH prices.

Overview
  • The average transaction value of ETH flowing in and out of exchanges decreased by 28% this week, hinting at larger players holding their positions.
  • We see increased average transaction sizes on USDT ERC20 inflows -  due to Tether minting 20M USDT on 09/30.
