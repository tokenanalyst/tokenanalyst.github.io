Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.
  • We added a new visualization of the daily net flow (inflow - outflow) below our weekly plots.

Bitcoin
  • Until 9th August, inflows and outflows were mostly on par (i.e. small net flow), with the BTC price stable.
  • From 10th August, inflows surpass outflows by 34% on average (i.e. positive net flows), with the BTC price declining.
  • On 13th August, BTC worth USD 94M flowed into the exchanges, with a BTC price drop of 7% within 24 hours.

Ethereum
  • Until 8th August, inflows surpassed outflows 34%, with the ETH price declining.
  • From 10th August, inflows and outflows were mostly on par, with the ETH price stable.
  • On the 13th August, BTC price dropped which often correlates with an ETH price drop. The ETH price remained stable because no significant net flow happened.

Stablecoins
  • Outflows were consistently larger than inflows.
  • A total of stablecoins worth USD 190M were moved out from exchanges.
  • Negative net flows in stablecoins correlate to decreased demand in crypto assets.

Overview
  • BTC and ETH exchange flows are stable compared to last week except for ETH inflows (-24%).
  • USDT outflows increased by 18%, while inflows decreased by 8%
