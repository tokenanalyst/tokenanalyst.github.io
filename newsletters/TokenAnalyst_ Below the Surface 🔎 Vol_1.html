Below the Surface

With this newsletter, we start a new series of weekly updates on the latest developments in the crypto universe. We leverage the latest on-chain and market data from the TokenAnalyst Pro API, and hope to give you all a deeper look into what's actually going on.

Highlights

  • New TokenAnalyst API endpoint: Miner hashrate & rewards
  • Bitmex investigation: Significant outflows on-chain
  • Exchange stats: Supply and demand of crypto assets
  • Network stats: Sentiment toward major coins, stablecoins, ERC20s and Binance chain coins

Miner Hashrate & Rewards API

  • We upgraded our fundamentals API with new miner hashrate and miner rewards endpoints for BTC and ETH.
  • These endpoints are a good proxy for 'decentralization' of the network.

Effects of the Bitmex Investigation

  • On 19th July, it was announced that the CFTC is investigating Bitmex.
  • On 20th July, our daily exchange flow bot showed that in the 24 hours since the investigation was made public, $85M worth of BTC moved out of Bitmex.

On-chain ripple effects

Key insights

  • The average value of transactions moving out of Bitmex tripled after the announcement of the investigation.
  • The large average transaction size indicates that whales are closing their trades in Bitmex and moving their funds out.

Trade Volume

Key Insights

  • The trade volume dropped by more than 50% in the three days following the announcement of the CFTC investigation. From a high of 5.4B$ on the 18th to a low of 2.6B$ on the 21st.

Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to a decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to an increased demand.

Key insight

  • After the price drop on 16th July, within four days on-chain volume to and from exchanges decreased by more than 70% on BTC, ETH and Stablecoins.

Key insights

  • The average daily inflow and outflow amounts, as well as the number of transactions, generally decreased this week.
  • Stablecoins outflow outpaces other token outflows by ~10%, indicating portfolio shifts into stablecoins.
  • The decreased number of transactions and average transaction value indicates a wary sentiment of the traders towards the current market.

Weekly Network Data

  • Analyzing general network data of a cryptocurrency allows evaluating the current sentiment of traders towards a respective coin.

Key insights:

  • The on-chain volume for major coins has taken a significant cut compared to last week. In fact BTC on-chain volume is 47% lower than the last week.
  • The volume drop for ERC20 tokens is even stronger, losing as much as 90% of the on-chain volume for some tokens. 
  • The transaction volumes for stablecoins have also dropped, although much less than the other coins. The number of active addresses decreased, i.e. less people are moving stablecoins this week. This indicates a wary sentiment towards trading this week.
  • The recently started Binance chain is still in infancy and the current statistics do not show a clear trend. We will be keeping an eye on it in the coming weeks.
