 History of Ethereum Mining and The Difficulty Bomb

 

Using TokenAnalyst PRO, @dangjeremy3 has written a short research piece on Ethereum mining and The Difficulty Bomb.

Check out our twitter thread for the full story and more visualizations.

Key Insights
  • The idea behind the difficulty bomb was to gradually increase mining difficulty at a certain block number with the goal to switch over to Proof of Stake.
  • The impact of the difficulty bomb is apparent as daily block count dwindled starting in mid-2017.

Weekly Exchange Stats

  • Analyzing transactions to and from exchanges can give a good indication of the supply and demand of a crypto asset.
  • An increase in outflow of a crypto asset from exchanges corresponds to decreased supply.
  • An increase in inflow of stablecoins to exchanges corresponds to increased demand.

Overview
  • We observe significant increases in trading activity for BTC and ETH this week. Likely caused by BTC and ETH price increasing by ~12% & ~26% respectively, between Feb 05 to Feb 11.
  • Trading activity for majority stablecoins are up significantly, indicating increased usage for these tokens as BTC & ETH prices increased this week.
